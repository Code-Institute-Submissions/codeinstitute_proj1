We are a Dublin based literary club with a largely Irish audience but of international appeal.

Our core philosophy is to seek to explore the issues of contemporary novels and literature, and how to use them to present the issues, in a manner appropriate to our audience, of our time and place. We also want to explore novel formats, culture as well as to explore novel trends, such as the changing popularity of print and television, the growing influence of the digital age. We believe that literary work, both on our digital platform and in print, will make a significant difference to Ireland's development and future. We intend to promote and celebrate our literary heritage. Our work in Ireland has been praised internationally. We hope that with our international reach and the right encouragement we can do just that.

Please walk around our website for more information and to contact us.

Our club has been formed in 1989, by Robert McDean, in Dublin and has since helped over 40 writers to publish their works and reach an international audience. Our original motto has been 'in love the best', and in 2007 we are finally announcing our decision to run the club and help to continue to grow our community.

In 2009 we have received rights to use one of the large conference rooms located at the Royal Dublin Society and ever since, our club has held its meeting on the premises.

In 2014 we have been awarded the Irish Cultural Award for our long and enriching activity, together with the publishing, in the same year, of more than 12 books in Irish language. "While the club is now in the process of going through a period of 'turning things around', it remains to be seen how we can improve the Club and help ensure that it remains up to the demands of its members." Mr McDean, former chief executive of The O'Clock News, was one of the founding members of the Club.
